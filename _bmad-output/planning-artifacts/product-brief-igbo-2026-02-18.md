# Product Brief: igbo

## Executive Summary

igbo is a comprehensive digital community platform designed to reunite and empower a geographically dispersed Nigerian cultural community. With members scattered across Nigeria, the US, Malaysia, Vietnam, and dozens of other countries — many of whom have no way to even find each other — igbo provides a dedicated digital home for cultural preservation, professional networking, commerce, civic governance, and meaningful human connection.

Unlike generic social platforms like Facebook or fragmented WhatsApp groups, igbo is purpose-built for this community's unique needs: a multi-tiered membership system with gamification, a points-based economy, hybrid communication (real-time chat + forums), built-in video conferencing with studio broadcasting, a full marketplace with shipping logistics, a dedicated job portal reviving the cultural apprenticeship tradition, democratic governance tools, and bilingual support. Starting with approximately 500 initial members and architected to scale to millions, igbo aims to become the definitive platform for community connection — and eventually, a solution other dispersed cultural communities can adopt.

## Core Vision

### Problem Statement

A large and vibrant Nigerian cultural community is scattered across the globe with no dedicated platform to connect its members. Community members living in Malaysia, the United States, Vietnam, and dozens of other countries are effectively invisible to one another — they don't know where each other are, and they have no way to discover, connect with, or support fellow members. The diaspora is fragmented, disconnected, and slowly losing the cultural bonds that tie them together.

### Problem Impact

- **Cultural erosion**: Without a centralized space for cultural exchange, traditions, language, and oral histories are fading — especially among younger generations growing up abroad
- **Lost connections**: Community members in the same city or country may never find each other, missing opportunities for friendship, mentorship, business, and mutual support
- **Fragmented communication**: Those who are connected rely on a patchwork of WhatsApp groups, Facebook pages, and word of mouth — with no unified directory, no governance tools, and no institutional memory
- **Untapped economic potential**: A globally dispersed community represents enormous professional and commercial potential that goes unrealized without a platform to facilitate trade, employment, and collaboration
- **Civic disengagement**: Without governance tools, the community lacks mechanisms for democratic decision-making, collective advocacy, and organized action on issues that matter

### Why Existing Solutions Fall Short

- **Facebook and WhatsApp** are generic social platforms — not built for community governance, cultural preservation, marketplace commerce, or professional networking under one roof
- **Existing groups are fragmented** — dozens of disconnected groups with no shared directory, no cross-group discovery, and no way for a member in Vietnam to find another member in the same city
- **No prior solution exists** — nobody has attempted to build a dedicated platform for this community before
- **Generic platforms offer no cultural context** — no bilingual support, no verification systems that reflect community trust, no points economy that rewards engagement, and no tools for democratic governance
- **Members fall through the cracks** — those not already in a WhatsApp group or Facebook community are simply unreachable, with no way to opt into connection

### Proposed Solution

igbo is a purpose-built community platform that serves as the digital home for this dispersed cultural community. It combines the best elements of modern social platforms, professional networks, e-commerce marketplaces, and civic governance tools — all designed specifically for this community's needs:

- **Community connection**: Real-time chat, group channels, forum discussions, and a member directory that makes every member discoverable
- **Cultural preservation**: A dedicated articles section, resource library, and bilingual support (English + native language) for knowledge transfer between generations
- **Professional empowerment**: A professional networking section with organizational email, and a dedicated job portal (on its own subdomain) featuring AI-powered matching, a full ATS, and a cultural apprenticeship revival program
- **Economic opportunity**: A full marketplace with shipping logistics, platform wallet, and points-based economy that rewards engagement
- **Democratic governance**: A comprehensive voting and governance system with elections, petitions, quorum enforcement, and transparent decision-making
- **Events and collaboration**: Video conferencing, studio broadcasting, project management with fundraising capabilities, and an events calendar
- **Scalable foundation**: Starting with 500 members, architected to grow to millions — with a future path to offer the platform as a white-label solution for other cultural communities

### Key Differentiators

- **"Digital home" for a diaspora community** — not another generic social network, but a purpose-built platform that makes scattered community members visible to each other for the first time
- **All-in-one ecosystem** — chat, forums, marketplace, job portal, video conferencing, governance, professional networking, and cultural preservation in a single integrated platform
- **Points-based engagement economy** — a gamified system with verification badges (Blue, Red, Purple) and points multipliers that rewards meaningful participation and converts to real economic value
- **Community-exclusive job portal** — a dedicated employment platform reviving the cultural apprenticeship tradition, with an innovative points escrow model that protects job seekers
- **Democratic governance built in** — voting, elections, petitions, and quorum-based decision-making baked into the platform from the ground up
- **White-label potential** — the platform is designed so that other dispersed cultural communities facing the same problem can eventually adopt and customize it
- **No prior solution exists** — igbo is a first-of-its-kind platform for this community, filling a gap that no one has attempted to address before

## Target Users

### Primary Users

#### 1. Chidi — The Diaspora Young Professional

**Profile:** 28-year-old software engineer living in Houston, Texas. Born in Nigeria, moved to the US for university and stayed for work. Misses the sense of community he grew up with.

**Needs & Motivations:**

- Wants to reconnect with his cultural identity and meet other Igbo professionals abroad
- Looking for a social experience comparable to Facebook but dedicated to his community — virtual events, forums, news feed, group discussions
- Interested in professional networking, mentorship opportunities, and career growth within the community
- Wants to stay informed about community governance and vote on decisions that affect his people
- Browses the marketplace for cultural items and products from home

**Current Frustrations:**

- Scattered across random WhatsApp groups that don't connect to each other
- No way to find other Igbo community members in his city
- Facebook groups are generic and lack governance, professional networking, or cultural preservation tools

**Success Moment:** Chidi discovers three other Igbo professionals in Houston through the member directory, joins a local chapter group, attends a virtual cultural event, and mentors a young person back home through the platform — all in his first month.

#### 2. Adaeze — The Young Person Back Home

**Profile:** 22-year-old recent university graduate in Lagos, Nigeria. Ambitious and hungry for opportunity but doesn't know where to look beyond local job boards.

**Needs & Motivations:**

- Seeking employment and career guidance — the job portal is a primary draw, but she's not limited to it
- Wants inspiration, mentorship, and guidance from community members who've succeeded abroad
- Needs to know what opportunities exist internationally without necessarily leaving home
- Eager to engage with the broader community — forums, events, projects, advocacy campaigns
- Wants to build her professional profile and get endorsed by community members

**Current Frustrations:**

- Doesn't know about opportunities available through the diaspora network
- No platform connects her to experienced professionals in her own community
- Feels isolated from the global Igbo network despite living in Nigeria

**Success Moment:** Adaeze finds a remote job posted by a diaspora member through the job portal, gets mentored by an elder through the apprenticeship program, and begins contributing articles about youth culture — realizing she doesn't have to leave home to access the global community.

#### 3. Chief Okonkwo — The Elder & Cultural Knowledge Keeper

**Profile:** 67-year-old retired teacher and community elder living in Enugu, Nigeria. Deeply knowledgeable about Igbo history, traditions, and language. Not very tech-savvy but passionate about preserving culture.

**Needs & Motivations:**

- Wants to pass down cultural knowledge, oral histories, and traditions to the younger generation before they're lost
- Interested in mentoring youth — both at home and in the diaspora
- Wants to participate in community governance and have a voice in decisions
- Needs a simple, accessible interface that doesn't overwhelm him with complex features

**Current Frustrations:**

- Sees cultural traditions fading among youth, especially those abroad
- Has no digital channel to share his knowledge with the broader community
- Finds existing social platforms confusing and not relevant to his needs

**Success Moment:** Chief Okonkwo dictates a series of oral history articles (with help from a younger family member), which become some of the most-read pieces on the platform. He participates in a governance vote and mentors three young professionals through the mentorship program — feeling his knowledge is valued and preserved.

#### 4. Ngozi — The Community Leader & Organizer

**Profile:** 45-year-old business owner and community chapter leader in London, UK. Energetic organizer who already runs local meetups and cultural events.

**Needs & Motivations:**

- Needs tools to manage her local chapter group — member management, event planning, content moderation
- Wants to organize events (both virtual and local), manage RSVPs, and broadcast to her group
- Needs to coordinate projects, fundraising campaigns, and advocacy initiatives
- Wants to influence and motivate her community through featured posts and announcements
- Interested in governance tools to run elections and votes within her chapter

**Current Frustrations:**

- Manages everything through WhatsApp, email, and spreadsheets — no unified tool
- Can't easily discover or recruit new members in her city
- Has no way to track engagement, manage content, or run democratic processes

**Success Moment:** Ngozi migrates her London chapter to igbo, sets up a group with a custom subdomain, runs her first virtual town hall with 200 attendees using studio broadcasting, and successfully fundraises for a cultural festival — all managed through one platform.

### Secondary Users

#### 5. Administrators & Moderators

Platform managers responsible for member approval, content moderation, governance oversight, and system configuration. They manage the approval queues, enforce community guidelines, configure RBAC permissions, monitor platform health, and handle disputes. They need efficient dashboards, bulk action tools, and comprehensive audit logs.

#### 6. Business Owners & Marketplace Sellers

Community members who leverage the marketplace to sell goods and services, post job listings, and promote their businesses. They need seller dashboards, listing management tools, transaction tracking, and visibility through the boost/promotion system. They bridge commerce and community.

#### 7. Guest Visitors

Prospective members who discover igbo through word of mouth, social media, or community leader outreach. They can browse limited public content — articles, event listings, and the About Us page — to evaluate the community before applying for membership. Their experience is designed as a window into the community that compels them to "walk through the door."

## MVP Scope

### Core Features (Phase 1 — 4-6 Months)

The MVP delivers the core community experience — making members discoverable, connected, and engaged. No commerce, no financial transactions, no mobile apps. Pure community-building.

#### 1. Authentication & User Management

- Member registration via admin-approved contact form
- Login with mandatory two-factor authentication (2FA)
- Three membership tiers: Basic, Professional, Top-tier
- User profiles with privacy controls
- Social media account linking
- Guest access to public content
- RBAC-based permission system

#### 2. Communication

- Real-time chat (direct messages and group chats, Slack-style)
- Basic group creation and management (Top-tier members only)
- Public, private, and hidden group types with configurable settings
- News feed with role-based posting permissions
- Commenting system with group-specific settings
- @mentions and threaded replies
- In-app and email notification system

#### 3. Content

- Articles section with admin and member publishing (based on tier)
- About Us page and organization information
- Constitution and governance document repository
- Guest-facing pages (home, about, articles, blog)
- Bilingual support (English + Igbo)

#### 4. Events

- Events calendar with RSVP and registration limits
- Basic video meeting integration using SDK (Agora or Daily.co)
- General meetings (all members) and group meetings
- Event notifications and reminders
- Recurring event support

#### 5. Basic Points System

- Points earning through likes and activities
- Verification badge system (Blue, Red, Purple) with points multipliers
- Points balance display and earning history
- Member dashboard with engagement metrics
- No wallet conversion yet (deferred to Phase 2)

#### 6. Navigation & UI

- Responsive web design (mobile-first)
- Personalized member dashboard
- Navigation menu: Home, About, Articles, Community, Events, Blog
- Global search and discovery features
- Member directory (searchable by name, location, skills, interests)

#### 7. Admin Tools

- Member application approval workflow
- Content moderation queue
- Member management and tier assignment
- Verification badge assignment
- Basic analytics dashboard
- Admin activity and audit logs
- Community guidelines enforcement

| **Marketplace**                      | Phase 2     | Commerce layer adds financial compliance complexity; validate community engagement first           |
| **Platform Wallet**                  | Phase 2     | Financial infrastructure (KYC, banking partnerships, compliance) needs dedicated focus             |
| **Points-to-wallet conversion**      | Phase 2     | Depends on wallet infrastructure                                                                   |
| **Mobile apps** (iOS/Android)        | Phase 2     | Responsive web serves mobile users initially; native apps follow once core experience is validated |
| **Professional networking section**  | Phase 2     | Organizational email, job board, mentorship — layered on after community is active                 |
| **Forum-style discussions**          | Phase 2     | Chat and news feed cover communication needs initially                                             |
